The Challenge

Egypt faces escalating challenges in electronic waste mismanagement. [cite_start]According to the Global E-Waste Monitor, limited access to repair tools significantly increases waste accumulation[cite: 322, 323].

690,000 Tonnes

of E-waste generated in Egypt in 2022 alone[cite: 262].
High Cost
Schools lack sufficient multimeters and soldering irons for STEM labs.

Our Solution

We propose a "Circular Economy" approach: The Solder-Meter. [cite_start]It is a hybrid device that combines a soldering iron and a multimeter into one chassis, built entirely from repurposed e-waste components[cite: 228, 229].

  • ✅ Cost-Effective: Uses salvaged heating elements and casings.
  • ✅ Integrated: Test your circuit immediately after soldering.
    • [cite_start]
  • ✅ Eco-Friendly: Prevents toxins from entering the soil[cite: 253].

Turning Waste into Heat

[cite_start]

We retrieved functional heating elements from discarded soldering irons and mounted them into a wooden handle for thermal insulation[cite: 283, 311].

  • Component: Salvaged Nichrome Heating Coil.
  • Power Regulation: Buck Converter used to step down voltage for safety.
    • [cite_start]
  • Thermodynamics: We calculated the energy required using $Q = mc\Delta T$[cite: 340].

Target: Reach melting point of solder (~190°C - 450°C) safely.

Precision Measurement

[cite_start]

We integrated a measurement circuit into the base chassis, allowing for real-time voltage and resistance checks[cite: 290].

    [cite_start]
  • Selector Dial: Salvaged from broken devices to switch modes[cite: 301].
    • [cite_start]
  • Probe Terminals: Reusable connectors from discarded meters[cite: 297].
  • Display: Recycled LCD screen for clear readings.

Target: Measure 220V AC with less than ±5% error.
